What is the prisoner's dilemma?

Imagine two poorly named brothers, A and B, have been arrested for a minor crime although the cops think they have done something much greater. To get a confession, the cops seperate A and B and give them both a choice- defect by ratting on the other, or cooperate by staying silent. The details go something like this-


  • If A defects (rats) while B cooperates (silence)
    • A goes free
    • B serves 10 years
  • If A cooperates (silence) while B defects (rats)
    • A serves 10 years
    • B goes free
  • If both cooperate (silence)
    • They both serve only 1 year
  • If both defect (rat)
    • They both serve 3 years

So what does it all mean?

The point of this thought expirement is to explain why cooperation might be advantageous when defecting often gives more reward. For creatures that hang out alone- being selfish is extremely advantageous. When it comes to creatures that group together, kindness and cooperation- or altruism- might be more rewarding.
